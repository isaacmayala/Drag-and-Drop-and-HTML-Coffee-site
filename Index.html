History of Coffee

The origin of coffee date back to 600 A.D. There are several legends about the origin of cofee, one of which states that in Eastern Africa, a shepard called Kaldi obseved that his goats were overactive when they ate cherry-red berries from a shrub. He tried to eat some of the berries, and found himself invigotated too. Soon new spread about the simulating affects of these berries, and monks started singing to the berries to stay awake during their long hours of prayer.

 

From Eastern Africa, coffee was transported to Arabia and then to Turkey, where they roasted the bens and boiled them and gave rise to a crude coffee that we use today. Soon, coffee spread to Europe, America and other continents, and now it is one of the most popular beverages enjoyed by multitudes of people all over the world.
